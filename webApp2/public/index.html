About

RoBox was created by a team of 7 students from Westlake High School — working together to create a very low-cost, take-home robotics kit. Setting out to do so in May of 2017, they taught their first week-long camp in late July 2017. This summer, they are putting on a second week-long camp at Qualcomm.

The RoBox kit aims to be extremely affordable, while still giving students the opportunity to build, program, and customize their own bot! The kit features two motors, a "Romeo BLE Mini" board, several sensors, and a drag-and-drop programming interface with a variety of programming challenges.

FRC 2468 Team Appreciate

FRC 2468 Team Appreciate of Westlake High School drives passion for STEM by engaging our community, assisting fellow teams, changing the political stage, and shaping STEM culture for future generations. After 10 years of inspiring STEM innovators and leaders, 2468 is committed to propagating and sustaining the mission of FIRST.

Chap Research

Chap Research is a student-led innovation program at Westlake High School that uses a project-based approach to teach students real-world technical and leadership skills. Chap Research has many active projects — all of which are student developed and led. RoBox is one of the most recent additions.
